## What Different Service Levels Actually Mean

Most shops offer 3-4 service tiers. Here's what they actually mean — in plain terms.

### 🟢 Bronze / Basic Service

**What it includes:**
- Gear adjustment (front and rear derailleurs)
- Brake adjustment
- Tyre inflation check
- Chain lubrication
- General bolt check

**What it doesn't include:**
- Brake pad inspection (they might note it but won't replace)
- Chain wear check
- Wheel trueness
- Bottom bracket play
- Headset adjustment

**Best for:** Bikes ridden less than once a week, stored indoors, used for short urban commutes. If your bike is more than 2 years old and you ride it regularly, this won't be enough.

---

### 🟡 Silver / Interim Service

**Everything in Bronze, plus:**
- Chain wear measurement (replaced if stretched)
- Brake pad inspection and replacement if necessary
- Wheel truing (minor adjustments)
- Cable inspection and replacement if fraying
- Bottom bracket feel check

**What it doesn't include:**
- Full bottom bracket overhaul
- Headset adjustment
- Fork service
- Handlebar taping

**Best for:** Road bikes ridden 100-300km per week. Gravel bikes ridden on mixed terrain every 2 weeks. Most recreational cyclists.

---

### 🔴 Full Service / Master Service

**Everything in Silver, plus:**
- Full bottom bracket overhaul (clean, repack, check play)
- Headset adjustment and re-grease
- Handlebar tape replacement
- Brake pad replacement (if worn)
- Chain replacement (if worn)
- Brake caliper centering
- Gear indexing from scratch
- Hub adjustment (quick check)

**Best for:** Every bike ridden more than 200km per month. If you're doing any kind of serious mileage — or riding in winter — this is the minimum your bike deserves.

---

### ⚫ Ultimate / Winter Overhaul

**Everything in Master, plus:**
- Full headset overhaul
- Fork lowering service (for suspension forks)
- Hydraulic brake bleed (if hydraulic)
- Frame bolt torque check
- Drivetrain deep clean and wax

**Best for:** Bikes that have been stored over winter. Bikes doing serious training mileages. Pre-event prep for races or big rides.

## The Hidden Costs Most Shops Don't Tell You About

These are the add-ons that inflate your bill at the end — unless you know to ask about them upfront.

### 1. Brake Pads
Most shops list "brake adjustment" as part of any service. But if your pads are worn, they won't do the job. Ask upfront: **"Are brake pads included if they need replacing, or is that extra?"**

**Typical cost:** £15–£40 per wheel for decent pads.

### 2. Chain Replacement
A worn chain doesn't just skip — it wears your cassette and chainrings faster. If your shop says "the chain is worn" and charges £30-60 to replace it, that's legitimate. A chain costs £15-40 to buy, so £30 labour to fit it is reasonable.

**Typical cost:** £30–£60 including parts and fitting.

### 3. Hydraulic Brake Bleed
If you have hydraulic disc brakes and they're feeling spongy, you need a bleed. This is specialist work.

**Typical cost:** £40–£80 at a specialist shop.

### 4. Bar Tape / Grips
Handlebar tape gets grotty over a season. Most full services include replacement, but budget services don't.

**Typical cost:** £20–£50 for tape. £30–£60 for grip replacement.

## Mobile vs Workshop Servicing — Which Is Better?

This is the most common question I get asked.

### Mobile Mechanic

**Pros:**
- They come to you — your bike stays in situ
- Good for basic adjustments and bolt checks
- No transporting a dirty bike in your car

**Cons:**
- Limited tools — can't do full bottom bracket work, headset presses, or wheel builds
- Working on the street isn't ideal — dirt, weather, distractions
- Usually charges more for convenience (call-out fee)

**Best for:** Interim services, bolt checks, pre-ride safety checks. Not for full builds or winter overhauls.

### Workshop

**Pros:**
- Full tool access — any job is possible
- Better environment — bike is secure, clean, properly lit
- Can get second opinions from other mechanics
- Usually cheaper (no call-out fee)

**Cons:**
- You need to get your bike there
- Turnaround can be 3-7 days at busy times

**Best for:** Everything serious. Full services, builds, upgrades, anything involving press tools or wheel work.

**My honest recommendation:** Use a mobile mechanic for quick jobs between workshop services. For anything that involves the bottom bracket, headset, wheel build, or brake bleeding — go to a workshop.

## How Often Should You Service Your Bike?

This depends entirely on how much you ride and in what conditions.

| Bike Type | Riding Frequency | Service Interval |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Road bike | 200km+/week | Every 3-4 months |
| Road bike | <100km/week | Every 6 months |
| Gravel bike | Off-road weekly | Every 3-4 months |
| Hybrid/commuter | Daily in all weather | Every 4 months |
| Hybrid/commuter | Fair weather only | Every 6 months |
| Kids' bike | General use | Once a year |

**The chain rule:** If you ride in rain, grit, or winter conditions — service every 3 months minimum. Salt and grit destroy chain, brake, and gear components fast.

**The storage rule:** If your bike has been sitting idle for more than 3 months — get it serviced before your first spring ride. Tyre inflation, brake cables, and brake pads all degrade sitting still.

## The Warning Signs Your Bike Needs Attention Now

Don't wait for your next service if you notice any of these:

### 🛑 Stop riding now:
1. **Chain skips under load** — stretched chain is grinding your cassette and chainrings
2. **Grinding from the bottom bracket** — the bearings are failing
3. **Headset knock** — your fork is loose in the frame
4. **Brake pads click or grind** — they're metal-on-metal and will destroy your rims/discs
5. **Any unusual noise from wheels** — spokes loosening or hub bearings failing

### ⚠️ Book in this week:
6. **Gear shifts are sluggish** — cable stretch or derailleur misalignment
7. **Brakes feel spongy** — cable stretch or hydraulic issue
8. **Tyre shows cords or cracks** — dangerous blowout risk
9. **Wheels feel unstable at speed** — likely wheel true issue
10. **Chain looks rusty** — rust accelerates wear on everything else
