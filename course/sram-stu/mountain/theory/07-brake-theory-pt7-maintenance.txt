Hydraulic disc brakes are high performance systems designed and tested as a whole to provide consistent and safe operation over a wide range of operating conditions. Use of non-system, aftermarket, or custom-made components can compromise the safe use and performance of the brake. It’s important to use components that were designed to work with their respective system. Hydraulic disc brakes contain many seals that are specifically compatible with the hydraulic fluid used in the system. Mixing even the smallest amount of other types of fluid can damage these seals which will seriously and permanently affect performance and function of the brake. Do not use any other fluid except what is specified for the system. Proper steps should be taken to maintain the integrity of the braking system and ensure the safety of the rider. A complete bleeding of the brake system should be performed per the brake manufacturer's recommended service intervals to maintain the performance of the braking system. All fasteners should be periodically checked for proper torque. Brake pads and rotors should be inspected for wear and contamination before every ride and replaced as necessary.
