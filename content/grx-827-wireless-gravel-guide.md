## Introduction

No wires.

That's the headline with the GRX RX827 Di2.

Shimano's first fully wireless electronic gravel groupset arrived with the same technology they proved in mountain biking with XTR and XT Di2 M9100 — and it landed in the gravel world with very little compromise.

If you've been putting off going wireless because you didn't want to deal with the complexities of wired Di2, the RX827 changes that equation entirely.

---

## What Is the GRX RX827 Di2?

The RX827 is Shimano's **fully wireless 1×12-speed electronic gravel groupset**.

Key facts:
- **1×12 speeds** — single chainring up front, 12 at the rear
- **Fully wireless** — no cables from levers to derailleurs
- **Electronic shifting** — every shift is powered, precise, instantaneous
- **Battery life** — up to 1,000km per charge on the rear derailleur
- **Charging** — via USB-C on the rear derailleur

---

## What Does "Fully Wireless" Actually Mean?

With a wired Di2 system (like GRX 825 Di2), you have:
- cables running from brake levers to derailleurs
- a junction box somewhere on the handlebar or frame
- wire management to deal with

With the RX827:
- the brake lever has a wireless transmitter built in
- it talks directly to the front and rear derailleurs
- no junction box, no cables, no wire management

**The result:** A cleaner cockpit, easier handlebar setup, and less to go wrong mechanically.

## 1×12 vs 2×12 — Which Is Right for You?

| | GRX 825 Di2 (2×12) | GRX RX827 Di2 (1×12) |
|---|---|---|
| Chainrings | 48/31 or 46/29 | 40T, 42T, or 44T |
| Rear cassette | 11-36T or 11-34T | 10-45T or 10-51T |
| Gear range | Wider overall | Huge rear range |
| Simplicity | Two chainrings | One chainring |
| Best for | Mixed steep terrain | Flat to rolling terrain |

### The 1×12 advantage

With a 10-45T or 10-51T cassette at the rear, you get an enormous gear range without the complexity of a front derailleur. No front derailleur to rub, no FD cable, no FD adjustment.

For riders who primarily ride gravel — not steep mountain climbs — this is actually the better setup. One chainring. One thing to think about.

### The 2×12 advantage

If you're riding mixed terrain where you need both a very easy climbing gear AND a high-speed descending gear, the 2×12 gives you a broader overall range. GRX 825's 46/29 with an 11-36T cassette covers both ends better.

## Who Is the GRX RX827 For?

**Buy it if:**
- You want the cleanest possible gravel bike setup
- You're building a new bike and want the latest tech
- You ride primarily gravel (not steep mountain passes)
- Wire management in a frame is a headache you don't want
- You value the XTR-level shifting quality in a gravel package

**Don't buy it if:**
- You need the lowest bottom gear for steep climbs — 2×12 gives you more range
- You're on a budget — wireless costs more than wired
- You're upgrading from a working GRX 810 or 820 — the gains don't justify it

## RX827 on a Custom Gravel Build

This is where it makes most sense.

When you're spending £2,500+ on a custom steel or carbon gravel frameset, wiring in a wireless groupset from the start eliminates the wire management problem entirely. The frame is built around the clean cockpit.

We spec'd a custom steel gravel build recently with RX827 — the builder routed the frame for brakes only (no wire ports needed for the groupset), and the result was the cleanest gravel bike we've built.

## The Wireless Advantage No One Talks About

With wired Di2, your front derailleur position is limited by cable routing. With wireless, you put the front derailleur wherever the frame design works best — not wherever a cable can reach.

This sounds minor. It isn't. On a custom build with irregular frame geometry, this flexibility matters.

---

## The Charging Reality

"Up to 1,000km per charge" sounds impressive. Real-world with mixed terrain and lots of shifting: expect 600-800km between charges.

The rear derailleur charges via USB-C. The front derailleur (if running a 2× setup — which the RX827 doesn't do) would have its own battery.

One USB-C charge every few weeks is the reality. No different from charging your Garmin.

---

## Honest Verdict

GRX RX827 Di2 is the best wireless gravel groupset Shimano has made. The 1×12 setup suits most UK gravel riding beautifully — and the lack of wires is genuinely transformative for how clean a bike looks and how simple it is to maintain.

**The trade-off:** You lose the 2×12's climbing gears if you're riding somewhere genuinely steep. Know your terrain.

**Best use case:** Custom gravel builds where the frame is built around a wireless setup, and the rider prioritises clean aesthetics and simplicity.
