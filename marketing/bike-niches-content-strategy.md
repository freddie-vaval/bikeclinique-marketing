## 🔗 Link Building Strategy

### Tier 1 — Local Citations (Foundational)
Get listed on every relevant local directory. These are easy wins:
1. Google Business Profile ← already done
2. Yell.com ← bike repair category
3. Thomson Local ← bike shops London
4. Streetlist.co.uk ← London business directory
5. Bike shops list on cyclinguk.org ← submit as member/recommended shop
6. Singletrack World forum ← vendor section
7. British Cycling club finder ← get listed as a club partner
8. Cycling Weekly directory ← submit shop

### Tier 2 — Guest Posts & Content Collaborations
**Target:** Cycling and bike blogs with UK audiences

| Publication | Domain Authority | Contact Type | Pitch Angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| road.cc | 72 | Contact form | "We wrote a guide on X — can we contribute?" |
| cyclingindustry.news | 55 | Editor email | Industry news + expertise |
| BikeRadar UK | 68 | Submit content | Equipment guides |
| CyclingTips | 65 | Pitch form | Technical content |
| The Guardian cycling | 95 | Commission | Already high DA, guest post or quote |
| BikeBiz | 58 | News tip | Industry angle |
| Cyclist magazine | 54 | Online editor | Expert commentary |

**Content to pitch for guest posts:**
- "The real cost of bike servicing in the UK" → road.cc
- "Custom bike builds: what you need to know" → BikeRadar
- "How to choose your first gravel groupset" → CyclingTips

### Tier 3 — Resource Page Link Building
Find resource pages that link to bike shops / mechanics / London services.

Search queries to find link targets:
```
site:.uk "bike repair" "useful resources" -competitor.com
site:.uk "bike shop london" "useful links"
site:.uk "cycling resources" "bike mechanic"
site:.uk "cycle club" "recommended bike shops"
site:.uk "mountain bike trails" "local bike shops"
```

Then outreach: "Hi, I noticed you're linking to [outdated link] — we'd love to be added to your resource list as a South London specialist bike workshop."

### Tier 4 — Forum & Community Links
These build authority and drive referral traffic:

1. **Reddit** — r/ukbike, r/londoncycling, r/gravelcycling, r/bikewrench
   - Answer technical questions with a link to relevant blog post
   - Don't spam — provide genuine value first, link naturally
   - Username: BikeClinique_London

2. **Cycling UK Forums** — cyclinguk.org/forum
   - Active community, people ask for shop recommendations
   - "I'm based in Clapham/South London and use BikeClinique" in signature

3. **Singletrack Forum** — singletrackworld.com/forum
   - Same approach — help first, mention service naturally

4. **Facebook Groups** — London cycling groups
   - South West London Cyclists
   - London Fixed Gear
   - Answer questions, mention BikeClinique when relevant

### Tier 5 — Local Press & Blog Outreach
Small effort, big authority:

1. **Wandsworth Council blog** — mention as local trader
2. **South London local blogs** — clapham.org, balham.org, brixtonblog.com
3. **University cycling clubs** — King's, Goldsmiths, Imperial — sponsor a fresher's event
4. **Cycling clubs** — Herne Hill Velodrome, South London Cyclists group

**Email template:**
```
Hi [Name],

I run [BikeClinique], a specialist bike workshop in Clapham. 
We specialise in custom gravel bike builds, groupset upgrades, and servicing.

We've been working with a lot of [club name] members lately and
```

## 📊 Content Production Checklist Per Post

For every blog post:

□ Target keyword in: title, first 100 words, H2s, URL
□ Internal links to 2 other related posts
□ External links to 3 authoritative sources
□ Featured snippet optimization (short answer in intro)
□ Images: 1 hero image + 1-2 in-body (optimised alt text)
□ Meta description: 155 chars with keyword + CTA
□ Schema markup: Article or FAQ
□ Social snippet: quote the best line for Twitter/LinkedIn
□ CTA: 1 clear next step (book/contact/download)
□ Canonical URL set
