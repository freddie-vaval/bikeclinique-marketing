# MODULE 1: THE FOUNDATIONS
## Your Workshop, Your Tools
*Lessons 1-5 | ~45 minutes*

---

### LESSON 1: Setting Up Your Workspace
**Who it's for:** Everyone — enthusiast or pro

**Filming:** Walk around a well-organised workshop. Freddie on camera.

**What to cover:**
- Minimum workspace needed (garage count? Flat space?balcony?)
- Why a dedicated space matters
- Lighting — why good overhead light + adjustable lamp is non-negotiable
- Flooring — concrete vs carpet, why it matters for dropped bolts
- The most important first purchase: a repair stand
- Why a bike stand pays for itself in 1 job

**Practical demo:** Setting up a mobile van as a workshop

**Checklist to give students:**
- [ ] Repair stand
- [ ] TORQUE WRENCH (non-negotiable, explain why)
- [ ] Basic tool kit list
- [ ] First aid kit (bleeding knuckles are real)

---

### LESSON 2: Your Essential Tool Kit
**Who it's for:** Beginners — what to buy first

**Filming:** Tools laid out on a mat, Freddie picking each one up

**What to cover:**
- Torque wrench (which one, why, what happens without it)
- Hex keys — the one set that covers 90% of bolts
- Plyers and cable cutters
- The chain tool
- Bottom bracket tools (which type for which system)
- Headset press vs hammer and drift (and why press is always better)
- The pump — track pump vs mini pump
- What NOT to buy (cheap tools that round bolts)

**Tool hierarchy:** Buy once, cry once vs cheap and replace repeatedly

**Cheat sheet:** Tool buyers guide PDF — which brand, which model, rough price

---

### LESSON 3: Understanding Bike Anatomy
**Who it's for:** Complete beginners

**Filming:** Freddie pointing at each part of a bike on a stand, explaining what it does

**What to cover:**
- Frame types: road, mountain, gravel, hybrid, folding
- Why frame geometry determines how a bike rides
- The drivetrain: chain, cassette, chainrings, derailleur
- Braking systems: rim brake vs disc brake
- Suspension basics: rigid, hardtail, full suspension
- The cockpit: stem, handlebars, seatpost, saddle
- Bottom bracket and cranksets: square taper vs press fit vs threaded

**Visual aid needed:** Diagram/animation of bike anatomy — can be a text overlay on the video

---

### LESSON 4: Workshop Safety and Professional Standards
**Who it's for:** Anyone going pro

**Filming:** Freddie at the bench, serious tone

**What to cover:**
- Torque settings — what they are, why they matter, what goes wrong
- Carbon fibre: how to handle without cracking it
- Hazards in the workshop: solvents, compressed air, heat
- Professional standards: how to treat a customer's bike
- Documenting the condition of a bike when it arrives (the protect-yourself step)
- Workshop insurance — what you need if you're working on other people's bikes

---

### LESSON 5: Reading a Bike — How to Assess Any Bike Quickly
**Who it's for:** Mechanics at any level

**Filming:** Freddie picking up a random bike and doing a 60-second assessment

**What to cover:**
- The 60-second check: tyres, brakes, gears, headset, bottom bracket
- How to identify frame type and components from across the shop
- Spotting crash damage: carbon cracks, bent metal, worn components
- What to look for before you quote: red flags that mean more work
- The pre-work inspection ritual that separates professionals from amateurs

**Exercise for students:** Do this on your own bike this week

# MODULE 2: MAINTENANCE ESSENTIALS
## The Weekly and Monthly Skills Every Bike Needs
*Lessons 6-10 | ~60 minutes*

---

### LESSON 6: Cleaning and Prep — The Right Way
**Who it's for:** Everyone

**Filming:** Freddie cleaning a filthy commuter bike

**What to cover:**
- Why proper cleaning is the foundation of everything
- Degreaser: what type, how to use, where NOT to use it (carbon frames)
- The chain cleaning ritual: brush, degreaser, rinse, dry, lube
- Frame washing technique: avoid high pressure near bearings
- Drying: why it matters, what happens if you skip it
- Post-clean inspection: what cleaning reveals (cracks, wear, loose bolts)

---

### LESSON 7: Tyre Maintenance and Repair
**Who it's for:** Everyone — most common issue on the road

**Filming:** Real punctures being fixed

**What to cover:**
- Types of punctures: thorn, pinch flat, glass, spoke puncture
- Tubeless: why it works, how to set it up, common mistakes
- Inner tube replacement: the right way, avoiding the pinch
- CO2 vs hand pump: which to use and when
- Tyre inspection: wear patterns that tell you about riding style
- When to replace a tyre (belt showing = replace)

---

### LESSON 8: Chain Care and Drivetrain Cleaning
**Who it's for:** Anyone who rides regularly

**Filming:** Chain on the chain tool, chain wear gauge being used

**What to cover:**
- Chain wear: how to measure it, when to replace (the 0.5% rule)
- Why a worn chain destroys cassettes and chainrings
- Cleaning without removing: the toothbrush method
- Full chain removal for deep clean: quick link vs pin
- Lubrication: wet vs dry, how much, how to apply
- Cassette and chainring cleaning: why it matters

---

### LESSON 9: Gear Indexing — Getting Your Gears Perfect
**Who it's for:** Anyone who rides gears

**Filming:** Freddie adjusting barrel adjuster, derailleur limit screws explained

**What to cover:**
- What indexing actually is (it isn't magic)
- The barrel adjuster: which way to turn and when
- High and low limit screws: what they do, how to set them
- B-tension: the adjustment most people skip
- Indexing under load: why you test while pedalling
- When indexing won't fix it (bent derailleur hanger = alignment tool needed)
- Quick diagnostic: if gears slip, what's likely to be the cause

---

### LESSON 10: Brake Basics — Rim Brakes
**Who it's for:** Road bike owners mainly

**Filming:** Brake pad replacement, brake adjustment

**What to cover:**
- Types of brake pad: compound, shape, road vs MTB
- Replacing brake pads: pad orientation, anti-squeal tricks
- Centring brake calipers: quick method
- Adjusting brake lever reach
- When brakes feel spongy: cable stretch vs hydraulic
- Squeaky brakes: diagnosing and fixing
