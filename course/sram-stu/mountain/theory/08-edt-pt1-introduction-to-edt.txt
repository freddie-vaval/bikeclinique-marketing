External drive trains are complex systems full of moving parts all working in sync with one another To ensure that the writer can efficiently pedal over a wide variety of terrain External drive trains are distinguished by multi geared cog sets and in some cases multiple Chainrings that use shifters and derailers to move the chain from one gear to another to achieve ideal cadence and torque Power input begins with the rider applying force into the pedals which is then transmitted through the crank arms the chain ring the chain The rear cog the driver and finally the wheel Torque and cadence are manipulated using different amounts of leverage available through multiple gearing options In this series of videos We'll take a look at how each of these components is constructed Specific features of each component that determine its performance characteristics and how all of these components work together.
